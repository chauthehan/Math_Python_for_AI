üî≤ Delimiters: Your Secret Separators

Remember how in Python we use quotes " " to show where a string starts and ends? Delimiters do the same thing in prompts‚Äîthey show the AI where different parts begin and end!

Why Do We Need Them?

Imagine you're reading a book where all the chapters run together with no breaks. Confusing, right? Delimiters are like chapter headings‚Äîthey help the AI know: "Okay, the instructions ended. Now here's the data to work with!"

üìä Formatting: Get Answers YOUR Way

One of the coolest things about prompts is that you can tell the AI exactly how you want your answer to look! It's like ordering a pizza and choosing if you want it sliced into 6 pieces or 8 pieces.

Popular Formats You Can Request

üéØ Putting It All Together

Let's build a complete prompt using all 4 pillars and delimiters! Watch how each part works together:

üìê Complete Prompt Blueprint

You are a creative children's book author who writes fun, exciting stories.

Write a short adventure story using all the elements I give you below.

###
Character: A brave little robot named Sparky
Setting: A magical forest
Problem: Sparky needs to find a lost kitten
###

The story should be exactly 5 sentences long and end with a happy ending.

Why This Works So Well

Each pillar does its job:

  • Context tells the AI WHO to be (a children's book author)
  • Instruction tells the AI WHAT to do (write a story)
  • Input Data gives the INGREDIENTS to use (Sparky, forest, kitten)
  • Output tells HOW to format it (5 sentences, happy ending)

The ### delimiters clearly separate the story elements so the AI doesn't get confused!
